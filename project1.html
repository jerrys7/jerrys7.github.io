Images of the Russian Empire: Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii Photo Collection

Purpose

The purpose of this project is to digitize and colorize the Prokudin-Gorskii Photo Collection. Sergey Prokudin-Gorksy was a 20th century photographer who attempted to capture the Russian Empire in color. He employed a principle of three colors where Prokudin-Gorsky divided the visible spectrum into three colors by capturing the information in three black and white photos, through a red filter, a green filter and a blue filter. These photos are then proejcted together and stacked together to create beautiful color photos.

The Algorithm

The core algorithm used here to align the projected images together was to build an image pyramid that consisted of scaling the image by a certain factor and then recursively determinign the best displacement in both the x and y direction. We then scale this displacement accordingly as we scale back up the size of the image to get our original image.
